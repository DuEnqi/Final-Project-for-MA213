Fright, and a feverish longing to escape the degeneration of Germans—an acute and kaleidoscopic tribe, tightened the grip on Nietzsche's throat. 'Amor Fati! he exclaimed, however, no god is spinning the thread of destiny, you suffer to transcend. Then why is this fragile body weakening my capacity to fight? Because we are aeronauts of the intellect.

I shall bellow at the abyss of uncertainty, but shall the void remain silent to me? O my expansive ego that in eternal pursuit of magnificence exists! Shall I call it Dionysian's evolution to the divinity? Ne, 'la philosophie ne suffit pas au grand nombre. Il lui faut la sainteté.…'

When a mediocre man dies, he shall be solitary. At the moment they turn to dust, there's the crack with the struggle of a life's last gasp. A clichéd imitation of a bygone era's collective imprint, reveling an assured outcome hit is. The ideal for which we live ultimately betrays life, how it resembles Wagner— 'the attitude is the end, drama and even music can never be anything else than means' ! In my heart, there dwells no artist, idol, nor cynic—nor shall it ever, 'I wish to be at any time hereafter only a yea-sayer!'

My whole work is ironic. ——Albert Camus

“Suicide, why?”

“I just thought of it, not thinking about it. It wouldn't be blasphemous just reasoning without a conclusion—— I'd say I am intellectually capable and therefore permitted to do so.”

“One could never be exempted from verdict by confessing his impotence. You are already dispelling God for being a determinant soul; might as well acknowledge that you simply have nothing beyond reason.”

“I am PASSIONATE about life and there is nothing rational about that. Whenever the bustling rays fade into the seeping dusk, I tremble at the thought that no shining shards of moments would ever occur that day.”

“Unlike you, people are presumed to live and carry on living. Or maybe mortality is just too horrifying, that you begin to seek meaning for the end yourself.”

“Then I have to refute that it is never the finite that bothers me, but when to terminate. The anxiety comes from the urgent quest: why can't now be the moment? All the possibilities the future unfolds shall redefine my being, yet how's that relevant to the present me?”

“To commit suicide is to deny. You'd be morally accused if you are indifferent about saving your loved ones from death. How could one not be expecting anything to happen tomorrow!”

“Unless there's the weariness of excessive repetition, suffering from detachment to the settings. Not once, in all those mock-heroic deeds, did I find even a whisper of contentment in my trivial, fleeting existence.”

“You are finally being honest about how greedy you are.”

“Shouldn't I be? Time is the eternal enemy of one who wishes to exhaust what is given. If no judgement is ever made, the pursuit of the experience quantity is all that is legitimate.”

“Then what if the world overwhelms you with inhuman denseness, what if your past collapses into the rupture of anonymity, will you be unjustified in being the subject?”

“I feel pity for your lack of courage in improvement. When I make my decision, I become a part of this cosmos, expanding to the fullest as I learn to love. We were consciously abandoned by the future. In that sense, I shall always be a stranger to myself.”

“But would the exploited potential be intrinsic? Is that harmony after all? How can you be sure that there is even a message to follow? The execution never comes, but you are still waiting till your demise. Be friends with that stranger.”

“...It is a perilous assumption. A philosophical deduction speaks for all human beings and you are responsible for that.”

“'Man is the only creature who refuses to be what he is.' Yet you objected to absurdity and killed yourself ontologically. Your hope for betterment strangles the freedom to exit the stage in grace. Think about the beautiful sun that is going to rise. Now the lucidity is nothing but tournament, isn't it?”

“I am NOT committing suicide, OK? There is still a dog for me to watch over, no other reasons are needed for my happiness. Why do you keep asking me all these? WHO ARE YOU anyway?”

“I am Camus.”

“Camus never says things that way; he tells people how to live.

“Of course, he doesn't. But YOU do.”
